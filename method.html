Method

In this study, I will be using a method known as Critical Interface analysis for Twitter analysis, which Sano-Franchini describes as covering the different categories of “theory, critique, and reflection” (Sano-Franchini, 8). Just like Sano-Franchini, my main goal for this assignment is to better understand how the user interface of Twitter plays a role in fostering or inhibiting user interactions and changing political and social sentiments online.

While I will be looking at the generic UX design on Twitter, I will also be choosing a trending current event using hashtags, and highlighting the importance of hashtags in searching for information and proliferating news content and opinions on Twitter. Since the Hashtag originated from Twitter, the use of Hashtags as a content highlighter is significantly more mature on that social media site than that of Facebook, which was mentioned by Sano-Franchini in her article.

Here are some of the questions presented by Sano-Franchini in her article(9) that I will also be using in my analysis of Twitter

  • Who is the target/primary user? Who is the secondary users or unintended users?
  • What kinds of content are presented through the interface?
  • What are the organizing logic of the interface?
  • What are the ideological and cultural values and assumptions imparted through the interface, whether through its content, its organizing logics, or the interactions facilitated by the site?

Here are some of the questions that I inquired myself while conducting the experiment

  • How does Twitter differ from Facebook in terms of User Experience? What does it do better? What does it do worse?
  • How were my experiences in conducting the study on Twitter different from Sano-Franchini's on Facebook?
